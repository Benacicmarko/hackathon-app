Why It Matters

  • Commuters — lost time, fuel costs, stress every single morning
  • Environment — CO₂ from thousands of half-empty cars on repeat
  • The city — road wear, parking pressure, slower public transit

People already want to share rides — they just don't have a simple, reliable way to do it daily.

This isn't a one-off road trip problem. It's the same wasteful pattern repeated 250+ workdays a year. Existing tools (WhatsApp groups, BlaBlaCar) aren't built for daily city commutes.

Our Solution

A daily carpool app that matches
drivers and riders on the same city corridors,
every morning — automatically.

No manual coordination. No driver approval step.
Seats fill up, a route is computed, everyone gets their schedule.

One sentence pitch: "Drivers post their commute, riders find the best match and apply, and when the car is full the app plans the route so everyone arrives on time." Emphasize daily/recurring — this isn't BlaBlaCar for long trips.

How It Works

  • 1 Driver posts morning intent — date, route, seat count
  • 2 Riders search by destination and arrival time — best matches ranked
  • 3 Riders apply — seats fill automatically (FCFS)
  • 4 Car full → route and schedule computed via Google Routes API
  • 5 Everyone sees pickup times, stops, and map
Walk through each step. Emphasize: no negotiation, no back-and-forth. The system handles matching and routing. Cancel policy: day before, seat reopens, re-route if needed.
